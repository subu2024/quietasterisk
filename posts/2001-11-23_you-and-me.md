---
title: "You and Me"
date: 2001-11-23
category: Love, Loss, and Time
featured: true
archived: false
excerpt: "A gentle poem about two people who remain together through the passage of time and the fading of everything else."
---

Time passes by  
World moves on  
People meet and depart  
In the end, that’s left, you and me

The entire world surrounds us  
The laughter and cries echo everywhere  
People known unknown, walk along  
In the end, leaving just you and me

The ironic twist, the friendly family  
Our own kith, our kin  
Everyone move into a new circle  
Leaving, but just you and me

The dawn of a new sun  
The end of a busy day  
In the cozy warmth of moonlight  
Lay, alone you and me

Staring into the vacuum  
Hanging onto sweet memories  
Watching the skin glow and wrinkle  
In the arms of time, lie you and me
