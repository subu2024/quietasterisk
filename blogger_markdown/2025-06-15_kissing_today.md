---
title: Kissing Today
date: "2025-06-15"
category: Love, Loss, and Time
featured: true
archived: true
---

Wrapped in the shroud of memory’s thread  
I straddle the living, the long-loved dead  
To chase the lustful arms of today  
Or dream with tomorrow, drifting away

Memories I try to leave behind  
Not bitter, nor sweet - just refined  
Stories once lived, now softly whisper  
Echoes adrift in a distant sky

Today, seductive, calls me near  
But asks for a price both sharp and clear  
"Unwrap the shroud," she gently pleads  
"And kiss me full, where nothing leads"

Tomorrow waits by the window pane  
A fleeting face in morning rain  
I run — she fades, then loops her play  
Returning quietly as bold Today

But vibrant Today, so fierce, so wide  
Keeps me shut from her glowing side  
Unless I lock the hallway tight  
And close the window on hoped-for light

So here I stand — no veil, no shield  
Between the ghosts and what may yield  
I kiss her not to cage or bind  
But to be, in breath, in heart, in mind  
And in her warmth, the window goes  
The hallway dims, but never fully closed
